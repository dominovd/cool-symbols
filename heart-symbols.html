How to use heart symbols

Tap or click any heart above to copy it to your clipboard, then paste it wherever you want — Instagram bio, TikTok caption, Discord status, text message, comment, document. The symbols are plain Unicode text, not images, so they render at the same size as surrounding text and inherit the font and color of whatever app you paste into. Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between ♥, ❤, and ❤️?
♥ is the Black Heart Suit from the playing-card set (U+2665), rendered as a flat text glyph. ❤ is the Heavy Black Heart from Dingbats (U+2764), also a text glyph but bolder. ❤️ is the same character with a variant selector U+FE0F appended that tells your device to render it as a color emoji instead of text. Different surface, same idea.
Why do some hearts show as boxes on certain devices?
Less common heart variants like ❦ or ❣ exist in Unicode but aren't included in every font. Older phones, older browsers, and some Linux distributions may show a fallback box. Stick to ♥, ♡, ❤️, and the colored emoji hearts for maximum compatibility across devices.
Can I use heart symbols in usernames?
Most platforms allow Unicode characters in display names: Discord, Instagram, TikTok, Telegram, Twitter/X. Some platforms restrict username creation to ASCII — check the field's rules. Display names and bios are almost always Unicode-friendly.
