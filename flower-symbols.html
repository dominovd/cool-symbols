How to use flower symbols

Click any flower to copy. Text-only floral glyphs (❀ ✿ ❁) work as decorative bullet points, section breaks, or accents around a word — ❀ welcome ❀. They sit naturally in the line of text without overpowering it. Color emoji flowers (🌸 🌺 🌻) are heavier and best used sparingly — one or two per caption for visual punctuation rather than every line. Combine with stars or hearts for popular aesthetic patterns: ✿ ♡ ✿, ⋆˚ ✿ ˚⋆, or ☘ ✦ ☘. The Hibiscus 🌺 and Cherry Blossom 🌸 are the most-used in 2025 social aesthetic.

Frequently asked questions

Are ❀ and 🌸 the same flower?
No. ❀ (Black Florette, U+2740) is a stylized abstract rosette from the Dingbats block, rendered as a text glyph in your font color. 🌸 (Cherry Blossom, U+1F338) is a specific real-world flower emoji rendered as colored artwork. Use ❀ for typographic decoration, 🌸 when you want the literal flower.
Which flower is the most popular aesthetic symbol?
In 2024-2026 trends, ✿ (Black Florette, U+273F) is the dominant text-style flower in aesthetic bios — it's smaller and more delicate than ❀, and pairs well with hearts and combining symbols. Among emoji, 🌸 leads for soft/kawaii aesthetics, and 🌺 for warmer or tropical themes.
Why does ☘ shamrock show in different colors?
☘ (Shamrock, U+2618) is technically a symbol character, not an emoji, but most modern systems render it as a green leaf anyway. Some older fonts show it as a monochrome outline. The fully-emoji-styled version is 🍀 (Four Leaf Clover, U+1F340), which is always green-colored on emoji-capable devices.
