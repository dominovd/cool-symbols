How to use zodiac symbols

Click any symbol to copy. Place your sun sign next to your name in a bio (Alex ♍ writer, Tokyo) for instant context. List your full chart compactly — ☉ ♍ ☽ ♓ ↑ ♋ — to share sun, moon, and rising. The planet symbols are useful in astrology readings (Mercury ☿ retrograde, Venus ♀ in Taurus) and double as alchemy/historical symbols in fantasy writing. Female ♀ and male ♂ symbols are also used as gender markers in personals/dating and in scientific contexts. The ⛎ Ophiuchus sign is supported in Unicode but most modern astrology systems stick with the twelve classical signs.

Frequently asked questions

What's the deal with the 13th sign Ophiuchus ⛎?
⛎ (Ophiuchus, U+26CE) was added to Unicode for completeness — astronomers note that the sun passes through the Ophiuchus constellation between Scorpio and Sagittarius. However, traditional Western astrology splits the sky into exactly 12 equal signs and doesn't use Ophiuchus. The symbol exists in Unicode mainly for sidereal-astrology and astronomy uses.
Why do ☉ and ☼ look the same?
☉ (Sun, U+2609) and ☼ (White Sun With Rays, U+263C) are visually similar but technically different glyphs. ☉ is used in astrology and alchemy for the Sun planet. ☼ is from the same Miscellaneous Symbols block but more decorative. Pick whichever your font renders more pleasingly — they both mean 'sun.'
Are planet symbols used outside astrology?
Yes. ♀ and ♂ are the standard gender symbols in biology, medicine, demographics, and sociology. ☿ Mercury and ♃ Jupiter sometimes appear in chemistry texts referring to the elements (mercury and tin, respectively, in old alchemical notation). The full planet set is also popular in fantasy writing for magical/elemental affiliations.
